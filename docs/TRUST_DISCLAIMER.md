## Core Principles

### 1. No Perfect Information Exists

Travel conditions change constantly. What was true yesterday may not be true today:
- Prices fluctuate
- Vendors relocate
- Areas gentrify or decline
- Events create temporary changes
- Construction appears overnight

**Our response**: We show confidence scores, not certainties.

### 2. Trust Is Earned, Not Assumed

New data enters the system at reduced trust:
- Fresh intel must be verified by others
- Conflicting reports reduce zone confidence
- Time erodes confidence (data decays)
- Multiple hazard reports trigger zone offline status

**Our response**: Transparent confidence scoring with clear explanations.

### 3. Degraded Data Is Better Than No Data

When confidence is low, we:
- Show clear warnings
- Reduce the prominence of uncertain zones
- Still provide the data (with caveats)
- Let users make informed choices

**Our response**: Graceful degradation, never false confidence.

### 4. User Safety Comes First

The kill switch exists because:
- Multiple hazard reports = real concern
- It's better to hide a zone than risk harm
- Recovery requires fresh verification
- We err on the side of caution

**Our response**: Automatic protection mechanisms.

## Price Data Disclaimer

Price submissions reflect:
- What **one person** paid
- At **one specific time**
- At **one specific place** within a zone
- With **their specific negotiation skills**

Prices vary based on:
- Tourist appearance
- Negotiation
- Time of day
- Vendor mood
- Events/holidays
- Currency fluctuations

**Never assume prices are fixed. Always verify locally.**

## Safety Disclaimer

Unmapped OS provides observations, not safety guarantees.

### We Cannot Know:
- Current crime conditions
- Political situations
- Health risks
- Natural disaster threats
- Personal safety factors specific to you

### Always:
- Check official travel advisories
- Register with your embassy
- Have emergency contacts
- Trust your instincts on the ground
- Have backup plans

### The Kill Switch Is Not Perfect
Multiple hazard reports trigger offline status, but:
- One critical incident can happen before reports
- Bad actors could spam false reports
- Real dangers might go unreported

**Your safety is ultimately your responsibility.**
